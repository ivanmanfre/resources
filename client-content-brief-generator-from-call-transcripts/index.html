Why This Workflow Matters

Here's what I see with agencies all the time: you've got client calls where golden insights drop - content ideas, objections to address, specific language the client uses, strategic priorities. But by the time someone gets around to writing the content brief, half of that context is gone.

The other problem? Consistency. One strategist writes briefs one way, another does it differently. The content team is constantly asking clarifying questions because the brief didn't include enough context.

This workflow solves both problems. It extracts the insights programmatically, applies consistent brief formatting, and can even match the client's tone of voice if you've got that documented. The AI does the extraction and organization work. Your team focuses on the creative strategy.

I built something similar for a consultant who was pulling content from his masterclass recordings and call transcripts. We embedded all that source material into a vector database so the AI had full context when generating briefs and lead magnets. Same principle applies here - you're giving the AI access to the actual words your client used, not your memory of what they said.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple pipeline:

  • Trigger: A webhook fires when a new call transcript lands (works with Fireflies, Otter, Fathom, or manual upload to Google Drive)
  • Client Detection: AI identifies which client the call belongs to and pulls their folder structure from Google Drive
  • Transcript Storage: The cleaned transcript gets saved as a Google Doc in the client's Transcripts folder (created automatically if it doesn't exist)
  • Brief Generation: Claude reads the full transcript and generates structured content briefs - extracting topics, language patterns, content opportunities, strategic context, and quotable moments
  • Output & Notify: Briefs are saved as formatted Google Docs, shared with your team, and a Slack notification goes out with a summary

Each brief includes: content objective, target audience, key messages, content angle, supporting points, client voice notes, format recommendation, and open questions for the client. Everything grounded in what was actually said on the call - no hallucinated data points.

Making It Multi-Client Ready

If you're running this for multiple clients, you need a lookup system for client-specific context.

Set up a client database with:

  • client_id - Unique identifier
  • client_name - Display name
  • voice_profile - Tone, vocabulary, style notes
  • content_pillars - 3-5 strategic themes
  • posting_cadence - How often they post
  • platform_focus - LinkedIn, Twitter, blog, etc.
  • recent_topics - Last 10 content pieces (to avoid repetition)
Add a lookup node after the trigger that pulls this context based on the client name in the transcript metadata.

Error Handling

Things will break. Here's how to handle it:

Transcript too long:
  • Gemini handles up to 1M tokens, but for Claude or GPT-4, you'll hit limits around 100k-128k
  • Add a conditional that chunks long transcripts or summarizes them first
  • For really long calls (2+ hours), consider extracting only the portions with the most speaker changes - that's usually where the good stuff is
Missing client in database:
  • Route to a manual review queue instead of failing silently
  • Send a Slack notification: "New transcript from unknown client - needs manual processing"
API failures:
  • Add retry logic with exponential backoff (n8n has this built in)
  • After 3 failures, route to manual queue and notify

Common Customizations

Add landing page generation:
One of the agencies I showed this to got excited about auto-generating Webflow landing pages from the briefs. That's a whole additional workflow, but it's doable - the brief feeds into a page generation agent, which outputs structured content that maps to your Webflow component library.

Connect to a knowledge base:
If you have a vector database with the client's past content, webinars, or training materials (like the masterclass embedding setup I mentioned), you can give the brief generation agent access to that context. Richer source material = more specific briefs.

Add competitor monitoring:
If the client mentions competitors on the call, trigger a secondary workflow that scrapes those competitor profiles and adds relevant insights to the brief.
