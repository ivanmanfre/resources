01 / Executive Summary

The Situation

VeriPro is the leading energy renovation operator in Spain under the CAE program, executing 40-45 installations per day with a target of 100+ per day in 2026. The operational stack was built rapidly over the past year and has reached a point where it needs to be cleaned up, documented, and restructured for scale.

With new external sales teams onboarding in the coming weeks and an expected intake of 80-150 new client entries per day, the current architecture presents critical risks around data permissions, automation reliability, and system fragility.

This proposal covers a paid audit followed by a phased implementation to resolve these risks and prepare VeriPro's infrastructure for industrial-scale operations.

03 / Key Risks

What Needs to Be Fixed

Critical
Monolithic CLIENTES Table
82 fields combining client data, installation tracking, digital signatures, commission calculations, communication triggers, and automation fields. Creates permission exposure, automation noise, performance degradation at scale, and onboarding friction.
Urgent
Shared Editor Access
External sales team leaders have the same editor-level access as the founder. With multiple new teams onboarding, this model cannot scale without risk of accidental data corruption or unauthorized changes to critical fields.
High
Unmapped Automations
Multiple Make scenarios running in production with no documentation, no centralized registry, and no error alerting. Silent failures mean missed confirmations, missing stats, and broken routes.
Medium
Broken Tables
LEADS (website form entries) and WHATSAPP (inbound bot entries) are poorly structured and need full rebuilds to support proper lead tracking and inbound request management.

07 / Important Considerations

How This Works

Live system, zero downtime. VeriPro executes 40-45 installations per day. All restructuring work uses a parallel-build approach: the new architecture is built and validated alongside the existing system before any cutover. Daily operations will not be interrupted.

Permission urgency. With new external sales teams onboarding in the coming weeks, the access and permission structure is the most time-sensitive deliverable. This will be addressed first within Phase 1, independent of the broader table restructure.

Existing freelancer work. The current automations and architecture were built over the past year by a freelance developer. The audit will document everything that exists before any changes are made, ensuring no functionality is lost during restructuring.

AI readiness. AI integration (Phase 3) is intentionally deferred until the data layer is clean and well-structured. Building AI workflows on top of disorganized data produces unreliable results. The right sequence is: clean architecture first, then AI.
