The Core Problem With "Automate Everything"

Let me paint a picture. You have a process - let's say it's something like generating compliance documents. Sounds simple enough. But then:

  • Kentucky has different requirements than Texas
  • Your enterprise clients need different formatting than your SMB clients
  • Some projects need 15 fields populated, others need 40
  • There are edge cases that only your most experienced team members know about
This is what I call a highly customized process. And the mistake I see most often is treating it like a standardized one.

When you try to automate a highly customized process all at once, you end up with one of two outcomes:

1. The automation is so rigid it breaks constantly - because it can't handle the variations
2. The automation is so complex nobody can maintain it - because you tried to account for every edge case upfront

Neither is good. Both waste money and time.

The Batch Approach: How Incremental Automation Actually Works

Instead of trying to automate the whole process, you automate in batches. Each batch is a contained piece that you can build, test, and validate before moving on.

Phase 1: Map the Variations

Before you automate anything, you need to understand what actually varies. I'm talking about documenting:

  • Geographic variations - what's different by state, region, country
  • Client-type variations - what's different by tier, industry, size
  • Process-type variations - what's different by project type, service type, product type
  • Compliance variations - what's different by regulatory requirement
You're not trying to solve these yet. You're just mapping them. I usually do this in a simple spreadsheet:
    • Process Step: Document header | Standard Version: Company logo + date | Variation 1: State-specific disclaimer (KY) | Variation 2: State-specific disclaimer (TX) | Variation 3: Enterprise client custom header
    • Process Step: Required fields | Standard Version: 15 core fields | Variation 1: +5 compliance fields | Variation 2: +3 compliance fields | Variation 3: +10 client-specific fields
This takes time. Don't skip it. Every hour you spend here saves you 10 hours of rework later.
