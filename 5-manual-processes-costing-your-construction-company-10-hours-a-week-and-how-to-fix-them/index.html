Checklist Specifications

Checklist Title: 5 Manual Processes Costing Your Construction Company 10+ Hours a Week - and How to Fix Them

Use Case: Self-audit for construction company owners, GCs, and ops managers (10-100 employees) to identify which manual workflows are eating the most time and prioritize what to fix first.

Section 1: Document Signing and Certification

Why this matters: I've watched construction owners sign 18-page documents page by page in Adobe, add certificates, enter passwords, save. Then do it again for the next document. And the next. Ten, twenty, thirty times a day. Some of these are SWPPPs, some are invoices, some are debit notes. The team that's "authorized to sign" often skips pages or misses signatures because the process is so tedious. That's a compliance risk on top of a time sink.

Check every item that applies to your company:

  • [ ] Someone on your team manually opens PDFs and signs each page individually (e-sign, click, next page, repeat)
  • [ ] You or an authorized signer handle 10+ documents per day that each need signatures on multiple pages
  • [ ] Signed documents sometimes get missed or pages get skipped because the process is so repetitive
  • [ ] Invoices, debit notes, and certifications each require separate signing sessions even though the process is identical
Time cost if most boxes checked: 5-8 hours/week

How to fix it: Batch PDF signing automation. Set up a workflow where documents land in a specific folder, get auto-signed on designated pages with the correct certificate, and save to a completed folder. The signer reviews a summary and approves the batch instead of clicking through every page. One approval instead of 200 clicks.

Section 2: Job Order and Project Data Entry

Why this matters: Every new job means someone is manually typing project descriptions, estimated areas, site addresses, and client info into your project management tool, then copying that same data onto Trello cards, job order forms, and bid documents. I've seen this with my own clients: someone changes a project description in one place, it doesn't update on the job order, and now you've got conflicting info floating around. Worse, address data gets entered wrong and the whole downstream chain (permits, job orders, client-facing docs) carries the error.

Check every item that applies to your company:

  • [ ] Project details (description, address, estimated area) are manually typed into multiple systems (PM tool, Trello, job order forms)
  • [ ] When someone updates project info in one place, they have to remember to update it everywhere else manually
  • [ ] Address or location data has come through wrong on job orders or permits because of manual entry errors
  • [ ] You rely on someone hitting "approve" or a checkbox before data flows to the next step, but there's no system enforcing it
Time cost if most boxes checked: 3-5 hours/week

How to fix it: Single-source project data with automated sync. Enter project details once. When you approve, automation pushes that data to your job order template, Trello card, and wherever else it needs to go. Change it in one place, it updates everywhere. Add a verification step ("I have confirmed civil plans are in the project folder") before the automation runs so nothing fires with bad data.

Section 3: Reading Plans and Extracting Project Info

Why this matters: A client sends you a 300-page civil drawing PDF. Your ops person has to open it (if their computer doesn't crash first), find the cover page, the vicinity map, the site plan (which might be called a "dimension control plan" or a "grading plan" depending on who drew it), and the erosion control plan. Then they manually pull the estimated area disturbed, sequence of major activities, and project description from those specific pages. Four drawings out of 300 pages. That's the actual work, and it takes forever.

Check every item that applies to your company:

  • [ ] Someone manually scrolls through large civil drawing PDFs to find the 3-5 pages that actually matter (cover page, site plan, erosion control plan)
  • [ ] Estimated area disturbed and project descriptions are pulled by hand from plan documents
  • [ ] Large PDFs (100+ MB) crash computers or take long enough to open that people avoid reviewing them promptly
  • [ ] The same plan data gets re-typed into SWPPPs, job orders, or permit applications by hand
Time cost if most boxes checked: 4-6 hours/week

How to fix it: Two steps. First, set up a dedicated folder where only the relevant plan sheets get uploaded (cover page, vicinity map, site plan, erosion control). Have your ops person pull just those pages instead of dumping the full set. Second, use AI (Gemini or Claude work well for this) to read those specific sheets and extract estimated area, project description, and major activities. The AI output feeds directly into your job order and SWPP templates. You review and approve instead of extracting and typing.

Section 4: Customer Folder and File Organization

Why this matters: Someone enters "Cleary Construction" for one project and "Cleary Construction Inc" for another. Now you've got two folders for the same customer. Multiply that across a few years of projects and your file system is a mess. People spend time hunting for the right folder, moving files between duplicates, and second-guessing whether they're looking at the latest version. It's not a technology problem. It's a consistency problem that technology can fix.

Check every item that applies to your company:

  • [ ] Duplicate customer folders exist because names were entered slightly differently across projects
  • [ ] Team members spend time searching for the correct project folder or moving files between folders
  • [ ] New job folders are created manually and sometimes end up in the wrong location or with inconsistent naming
  • [ ] There's no automated check to catch duplicate or misspelled customer/project names before folders are created
Time cost if most boxes checked: 2-3 hours/week

How to fix it: Automated folder creation with fuzzy name matching. When a new project kicks off, the automation searches existing customer names before creating a new folder. If "Cleary Construction" already exists and someone types "Cleary Construction Inc," it flags the match and asks which one to use instead of silently creating a duplicate. Your team fixes edge cases manually, but the system catches 90% of the duplicates before they happen.

Section 5: Lead Qualification and Data Processing

Why this matters: Your business development person pulls leads from Apollo or whatever tool you use, then manually scores them, weeds through the bad ones, cross-references against your CRM, and formats the data for outreach. Before any automation, teams I've worked with were wading through hundreds of leads trying to figure out which ones were worth a call. After: sort by lead score, call the top of the list. The data quality difference is real.

Check every item that applies to your company:

  • [ ] Leads are manually reviewed and scored one by one instead of being auto-ranked by fit criteria
  • [ ] Someone exports data from one tool (lead gen, estimating, timekeeping) and manually reformats it as a CSV or spreadsheet for upload into another system
  • [ ] Labor cost data, sales reports, or timesheet info requires manual column mapping before it can be used in reporting tools
  • [ ] Your team spends time on leads that turn out to be low quality because there's no automated filtering
Time cost if most boxes checked: 3-5 hours/week

How to fix it: Automated lead scoring and data pipeline. Connect your lead source to your CRM with a scoring layer in between. Leads get ranked automatically based on your criteria. Your team starts at the top of the list instead of guessing. For data processing (timesheets to labor reports, estimates to bid docs), build a simple automation that maps the columns and formats the output so you skip the spreadsheet gymnastics entirely.

Implementation Priority

If you checked boxes in multiple sections, here's where to start:

Priority 1: Document Signing (Section 1) - Highest time savings per dollar spent. The process is identical every time, which makes it the easiest to automate. If you're signing 10+ documents a day, fix this first.

Priority 2: Job Order Data Entry (Section 2) - This is where errors compound. Bad data in one place spreads everywhere. Automating the single-source-of-truth flow prevents downstream problems and saves time.

Priority 3: Plan Reading and Data Extraction (Section 3) - Bigger lift to set up because it involves AI reading documents, but the payoff is large if your team processes plans regularly. Start with the folder discipline (only upload the pages you need), then add AI extraction.

Your Score

Count your total checked boxes:

  • 0-4 boxes: You're in decent shape. Pick the one or two that bug you most and fix those.
  • 5-10 boxes: You're probably losing 10-15 hours a week across these processes. Start with Priority 1 and work down.
  • 11-18 boxes: Yeah, you're bleeding time. The good news is that most of these fixes are straightforward and pay for themselves within a month. Start with the section where you checked every box.
